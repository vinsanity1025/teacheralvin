Round 1: Target Toss Evaluation

SCORING RUBRIC (10 Points)

Criteria Excellent Very Good Fair Poor
Classification (4 pts) (4) Correctly and flawlessly identifies accuracy & precision. (3.5) Correctly identifies one concept but misjudges the other. (3) Misidentifies both, but recognizes a distinct visual pattern. (2.5) Completely incorrect or no definitive classification.
Clarity of Explanation (6 pts) (6) Explicitly links spread to precision & distance to accuracy with terminology. (5.5) Explains correctly but justification is generic without direct data reference. (5) Vague explanation; confuses definitions of precision/accuracy. (4.5) No scientific justification or relies on non-scientific reasoning.
Procedure: Draw a circular target with a clearly marked center (representing the true value) on the chalkboard using chalk. Each group member must stand exactly 3 meters away from the board. One by one, throw a small, damp paper wad at the target to leave a temporary mark.

Documentation Requirement: Take a clear photograph of the final cluster of marks on the board. You will be required to upload this image to the designated Google Drive folder at the end of the activity.

Round 3: Pendulum Time Trials

SCORING RUBRIC (10 Points)

Criteria Excellent Very Good Fair Poor
Math Process & Doc. (5 pts) (5) Documents raw data table, mean calculation, deviations, & variance formula. (4.5) Shows most steps but skips intermediate individual deviations. (4) Work is disorganized; intermediate steps proving formula are missing. (3.5) Illegible, missing entirely, or wrong formula utilized.
Accuracy & Format (5 pts) (5) Mean/variance flawless. Includes correct units & sig figs. (4.5) Minor arithmetic error, but procedural logic is sound. (4) Major procedural calculation error (e.g., dividing by N instead of N-1). (3.5) Variance is completely incorrect due to cascading errors; units absent.
Procedure: Construct a simple pendulum using a string measuring exactly 50 centimeters and a heavy mass. Displace the pendulum slightly and release it. Use a stopwatch to measure the time it takes for the pendulum to complete exactly 5 full oscillations (swings back and forth). Repeat this process for a total of 5 independent trials.

Documentation Requirement: On a separate sheet of paper, calculate the mean of your times and the sample variance. Take a clear photograph of your complete, step-by-step mathematical solution to upload to the Drive later.
